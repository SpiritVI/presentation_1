Misinformation is false information that is shared inadvertently, without meaning to cause harm. The terms misinformation and disinformation are often used interchangeably.

When Thales meets IA

Learning to doubt in order to approach the truth

Lexicon:


  1. Senses in latin mean perception, feeling, sensation, sense. Now, we call this an induction reasoning.
  2. Ratio mean reason. The term in our contemporary times is "deduction"

Nowadays, we call this reasoning by induction and deduction. The first one consists of reflecting from particular to the universal, the second one is the opposite.

Exemple 1:
"Manilius says : they observed the appearance of the whole night sky and watched every star return to its original place […] by doing this repeatedly, they built up their knowledge."
Exemple 2:
"The Aetna is an anonymous text that explains how volcanoes work. Its unknown author warns readers about two potential sources of misinformation: other authors and other people."

The courage to doubt

Exemple 3:
"In On the Nature of Things, Roman philosopher Lucretius proposed three different explanations for solar eclipses:

  1. the Moon passing in front of the Sun
  2. some other opaque body passing in front of the Sun, or
  3. the Sun's light temporarily growing dim for some reason."
